## Distributed Systems: Concepts and Design

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Authors | COULOURIS, G; DOLLIMORE, J; KINDBERG, T; BLAIR, G. |
| Publication Year | 2013 |
| Edition | 5th edition. |

### Description

The Internet and the Web keep growing and have an increasingly greater impact on various aspects of our society. From the standpoint of developing distributed systems, which interconnect computers and other devices, this growth imposes new demands in terms of architecture, algorithms, design requirements, variety of applications and of workloads. The 5th edition of this best-seller continues to be a comprehensive source of knowledge about the principles and the practice of distributed systems, with modern case studies that illustrate their design and development and exercises at the end of each chapter that help to reinforce the content covered. Highlights of the 5th edition:• New chapters: indirect communication, distributed objects and components, and distributed systems design.• New topics: cloud computing, network and operating system virtualization, unstructured peer-to-peer systems, tuple spaces, loose coupling in Web services, and mobile and ubiquitous computing.• New case studies: JavaSpaces, JMS, WebSphere, Messa Queues, Entreprise JavaBeans, Skype, Gnutella, TOTA, L2imbo, BitTorrent, End System Multicast, Google infrastructure.

## Distributed Systems: Principles and Paradigms 

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Authors | Andrew S. Tanenbaum, Maarten Van Steen. |
| Publication Year | 2007 |
| Edition | 2nd edition. |
| ISBN | 9788576051428 |

### Description

In this second edition, improved and updated, Tanenbaum and Van Steen present the operating details of distributed systems, as well as the underlying technologies. By separating principles from paradigms, the authors show how the principles relate to one another, discussing four different classes of distributed systems, which allows an even more comprehensive understanding of the content presented.
