### Description

<p align="justify">
Written by the renowned professors Douglas C. Montgomery and George C. Runger, this seventh edition of Applied Statistics and Probability for Engineers includes the essential content of the subject, common to the various Engineering specializations. The authors' premise was to conduct extensive research with professors so that the book would encompass the theory needed to meet classroom demands. As a result, the bootstrap technique was included, in addition to the descriptive and analytical methods needed by those who deal with variability in observing data. This seventh edition includes new exercises, created from real, everyday situations of engineers, which are available online, as well as new chapters, examples, graphs, practical interpretations, and computationally intensive methods. As a highlight, at the beginning of every chapter, Montgomery and Runger created the topic "Learning Objectives", explaining to the reader what to expect from the chapter and what they will be able to understand and do by the end of the study. Although mathematics is fundamental in Statistics and Probability, the authors do not intend merely to provide the theory, but to broaden the methodology and show how to apply it. 
</p>

### Description

<p align="justify">
The best-selling book in the field, the 10th edition of Basic Statistics confirms its great success with extensive revision, updating, and expansion work, which had enriching suggestions from professors and readers. New features of this edition: New chapter on various topics in time series. Introduction to multiple linear regression in Chapter 16. Specific modifications throughout the work. The book is divided into three parts. The first deals with the analysis of one-dimensional and two-dimensional data, with special attention to graphical methods. The second part presents the basic concepts of probability and random variables. Finally, the third part studies the main topics of statistical inference, in addition to some special themes, such as regression models. Complete and didactic, Basic Statistics is the most suitable book for students and professionals from various fields of knowledge and stands out for being the only work with a section in every chapter that teaches how to apply the theory through the computational packages Minitab®, Excel®, Splus®, and packages from the free R® Repository. At the end of the book, the authors provide several datasets for download on the Saraiva Conecta platform.
