## Algorithms

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Authors | DASGUPTA, S.; PAPADIMITRIOU, C.; VAZIRANI, U. |
| Publication Year | 2010 |
| Edition | 1st edition. |
| ISBN | 9788577260324 |

### Description

Extensively tested for about a decade in university courses, and adapted to the needs of students, Algorithms explains the fundamentals of algorithms in a historical, easy-to-read, and easy-to-assimilate way. The emphasis is on understanding the mathematical idea behind each algorithm, in an intuitive, rigorous way, without being formal.

## Algorithms - Theory and Practice

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Authors | CORMEN, T.; LEISERSON, C.; RIVEST, R.; STEIN, C. |
| Publication Year | 2024 |
| Edition | 4th edition. |
| ISBN | 9788595159907 |

### Description

A worldwide best-seller on the subject, Algorithms – Theory and Practice reaches a new edition! With increasingly advanced technological development, knowing how to work with algorithms has become an important differentiator for students and professionals, because, nowadays, algorithms are applicable in various situations – in the areas of technology, engineering, health, finance, education, among others –, from the simplest to the most complex tasks. Thus, with its didactic approach and unique combination of rigor and breadth, the purpose of this book is to make algorithms an accessible tool for readers of all levels of knowledge. The work combines theory and practice to provide a solid understanding of the fundamental principles of algorithms, while also providing practical examples and complexity analyses.

In addition, it covers a variety of algorithms, data structures, and algorithm design techniques. Fully updated, this fourth edition presents the following new features:
<ul>
<li>New chapters on matchings in bipartite graphs, online algorithms, and machine learning;</li>
<li>New material on topics such as solving recurrence equations, hash tables, potential functions, and suffix arrays;</li>
<li>Of the nearly 1,000 exercises available, 140 are new; and of the 162 problems, 22 are new;</li>
<li>Updated notes and bibliography to reflect developments in the field.</li>
</ul>

The chapters are relatively independent, and each one presents an algorithm, a design technique, an application area, or a related topic. In addition, the algorithms are described in a simple, quite objective way and in pseudocode designed to be read by any user with basic programming knowledge, regardless of the programming language used. The content was planned for undergraduate and graduate courses in algorithms or data structures, as well as for specialization and updating of professionals in the field.

## How to Think About Algorithms

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Authors | Jeff Edmonds |
| Publication Year | 2010 |
| Edition | 1st edition. |
| ISBN | 9788521617310 |

### Description

The goal in this work is not to present the complete package, neatly wrapped and clean, but to slowly and deeply walk through each stage of the development of algorithms. Many false starts have been added, in the hope that this will help students develop algorithms on their own. People who develop algorithms have different ways of thinking, as well as an intuition that, generally speaking, is not taught. The basic premise of How to Think About Algorithms is that this cannot be taught, but has to be discovered individually. This book attempts to teach students to think like an algorithm designer. Jeff Edmonds has written an entertaining book, organized into blocks, each containing a title and a single thought. The author focuses on the structure and proof of correctness of iterative and recursive meta-algorithms and, within these, of greedy and dynamic programming meta-algorithms. By learning these and their proofs of correctness, one can easily understand most algorithms. The challenge is that thinking about meta-algorithms requires quite a lot of abstraction. Analogies, paradigms, and different notations are presented, within which it is possible to develop and think about algorithms.
