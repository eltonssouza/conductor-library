### Description

Since students taking this subject for the first time often find it very abstract and do not see how they can use the basic concepts, the authors of the book — experienced professors at UNICAMP — took care to emphasize the use of the concepts throughout the text. Being an introductory course, the prerequisites for using this book are the Mathematics topics normally studied in High School.

| Publication Year | 2011 |
| Edition | 4th edition. |
| ISBN | 9788577808335 |

### Description

This book provides an introduction to Linear Algebra. It follows the Schaum's Outline didactic approach: each chapter begins with definitions, principles, and theorems, and with descriptive and illustrative material, advances to a set of graduated solved exercises, and ends with supplementary problems for a complete review. It includes Appendices with modules and polynomials over a field, tensor and exterior products of vector spaces, and the Moore-Penrose generalized inverse.

## Linear Algebra 

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Authors | LANG, S. |
| Publication Year | 2003 |
| Edition | 1st edition. |
| ISBN | ‎9788573932539 |

### Description

Linear Algebra is aimed at a one-term course at the junior or senior level. It begins with an exposition of the basic theory of vector spaces and proceeds to explain the fundamental theorems for linear maps, including eigenvectors and eigenvalues, Hermitian and quadratic forms, diagonalization of matrices, and unitary, Hermitian, and symmetric linear maps, triangulation, and the Jordan canonical form. The book also includes a useful chapter on convex sets and the finite-dimensional Krein-Milman theorem. The presentation is geared toward the student who has already had some contact with the elementary theory of matrices, determinants, and linear maps. However, the book is, of course, self-contained. In this new edition, several passages of the book were rewritten and reorganized, and new exercises were added.
