## Modern Operating Systems

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Authors | Tanenbaum, Andrew S. |
| Publication Year | 2024 |
| Edition | 5th edition. |
| ISBN | 9788582606162 |

### Description

This new edition addresses the latest developments in operating system technologies. The case studies explore popular operating systems, always contextualized to our reality. In a clear and entertaining way, the authors describe the concepts that operating system designers need to master, such as processes, threads, memory management, file systems, I/O (input/output), deadlocks, interface design, multimedia, performance trade-offs, and trends in operating system design. It is also worth highlighting a new chapter on Windows 11, the updated security chapter with more focus on topics that are directly relevant to operating systems, and the emphasis on flash-memory-based solid state drives (SSDs).

## Operating System Concepts

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Authors | Abraham Silberschatz |
| Publication Year | 2015 |
| Edition | 9th edition. |
| ISBN | 9788521629399 |

### Description

Operating System Concepts, now in its 9th edition, is divided into 20 chapters, made up of records of experiences lived by the authors in a constantly transforming universe. In this book, it is possible to have an in-depth approach to subjects such as mobile systems and cloud computing. The success of the work begins in its very conception, when the authors understand that operating systems are an essential part of any computing system. Thus, the content was designed to offer a broad theoretical foundation to students and professionals in the field. The fact that the authors work in detail with Linux and Windows – systems about which some users still have many doubts – makes the work even more distinctive, unique, and interesting. It also provides a thorough analysis of the systems used in smartphones, which are the major highlight of the moment in the sector. The contents are addressed in a singular way. In addition to concrete conceptualization, there are still dozens of exercises and 20 more new problems and programming projects. Those who wish to broaden their knowledge in this area will find in Operating System Concepts an excellent tool.

## Operating Systems Architecture: Including Exercises with the SOSIM Simulator and ENADE Questions

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Authors | Machado, Francis Berenger; Maia, Luiz Paulo. |
| Publication Year | 2013 |
| Edition | 5th edition. |
| ISBN | 9788521622109 |

### Description

The study of operating systems gains importance as various computing professionals need this knowledge to carry out their activities, such as system administrators, concurrent application programmers, and security managers. Another important factor is the relationship of operating systems within data communication networks, which makes their study necessary for network administrators. Operating Systems Architecture addresses the architecture and operation of multiprogrammable operating systems in a current, comprehensive, and didactic manner. Its content is aimed at students and IT professionals in all areas. As a basic prerequisite for reading it, only some knowledge of computer organization and data structures is needed. Whenever the use of programs is necessary, the Pascal programming language will be used, due to its clarity and fluency in the academic environment, as well as the Java language, due to its wide acceptance in the market.
