## Numerical Methods for Engineering

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Authors | Steven C. Chapra (Author) and Raymond P. Canale |
| Publication Year | 2016 |
| Edition | 7th edition. |
| ISBN | 9788580555684 |

### Description

Written by renowned authors, Numerical Methods for Engineering presents an extensive range of numerical methods, such as the treatment of optimization and differential equations. With simple, practice-oriented explanations, it features examples, case studies, and problems designed according to engineering practice, including emerging areas such as bioengineering.This edition maintains its focus on the appropriate use of computational tools, bringing meticulous discussions about their mathematical foundations. It also provides pseudocode for the algorithms of the numerical methods and an overview of popular software packages, such as MATLAB, Excel, and MathCAD.Winner of the best textbook award from the American Society for Engineering Education, this is an indispensable resource for Engineering courses and others in the Exact Sciences area, such as Chemistry, Physics, Mathematics, and Computing.

## Numerical Calculus

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Authors | Décio Sperandio, João Teixeira Mendes and Luiz Henry Monken |
| Publication Year | 2014 |
| Edition | 2nd edition. |
| ISBN | 9788543006536 |

### Description

With current computational resources, numerical methods have become indispensable in the training of scientists and engineers. Meeting this need, Numerical Calculus presents the most important mathematical procedures for the analysis of complex mathematical models, originating from the most varied areas of knowledge. In the work, the topics are described in a way that always has a clear mathematical basis. The arrangement of the topics facilitates a growing understanding of the most frequent approaches in numerical problems, and many step-by-step solved examples aid the learning of the numerical schemes. To complete it, exercises at the end of each chapter allow the reader to test their knowledge and explore the theoretical content developed.
